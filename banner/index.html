Important Safety Information Scroll ▼
Boxed Warning

Ion drive co-exposure within 6 hours of Stellaria administration has been associated with prolonged QT interval and, in rare cases, Torsades-class arrhythmia during cryo-induction.

Indication

Stellaria is indicated as hibernation-assist therapy in licensed deep-space personnel ≥ 21 years undergoing planned cryo-induction on missions of 90 days or longer.

Contraindications

Known hypersensitivity to morphanidine-C; active cryo-sensitization syndrome (CSS); uncorrected Grade II telomeric instability; Class-III temporal stabilizers; pregnancy.

Warnings and Precautions

Monitor core temperature for 90 minutes post-thaw. Neuro-vestibular effects, including transient orientation loss, may occur on re-animation; no EVA duties for 120 minutes post-thaw.

Adverse Reactions

Most common adverse reactions (≥ 5%) were dry mouth, mild paresthesia, transient temporal displacement, metallic taste, and mild photosensitivity.

Drug Interactions

Avoid Class-III temporal stabilizers and MAOIs within 14 days. Caffeine analogs above 400 mg/day caf-eq may blunt induction onset.
